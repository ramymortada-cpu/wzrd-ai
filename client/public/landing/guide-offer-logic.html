The product isn't the problem. The offer is. The way you package, price, and present what you sell determines whether people buy — not the quality of what's inside.

This guide covers the 4 pillars of offer logic. No selling — just the frameworks that consultants and pricing strategists actually use.

The 4 Pillars of Offer Logic

The Offer Logic Framework

Pillar 1: ICP Clarity

ICP stands for Ideal Customer Profile. It answers: Who gets the MOST value from what you offer? Not "everyone who might buy" — the one who SHOULD buy.

When your ICP is fuzzy, everything downstream breaks: messaging tries to please everyone (resonates with no one), pricing can't be premium (not specialist enough), sales feel like pushing instead of pulling.

The Test: Can you fill this? 'I help [SPECIFIC PERSON] who has [SPECIFIC PROBLEM] achieve [SPECIFIC OUTCOME].' If any part is vague, your ICP needs work.

Pillar 2: Package Structure

Research in behavioral economics shows that too many choices paralyze the customer. The famous jam study: 24 options → 3% conversion. 6 options → 30%. The most effective structures use 2-3 tiers: Good / Better / Best. The middle becomes the anchor.

Pillar 3: Pricing Logic

Most businesses price by cost-plus or by copying competitors. Both are wrong. Price communicates value. A $50 session and a $5,000 session could contain the same advice — but the $5,000 one is perceived as more valuable and attracts more committed clients.

The Anchoring Effect (Kahneman): The first price seen becomes the reference. Show premium first → mid-tier feels like a bargain. Show cheapest first → everything else feels expensive. Order matters.

Self-Assessment Checklist

  • I can describe my ideal customer in one specific sentence
  • I have 2-3 packages (not 6+)
  • A stranger can understand my offer in under 30 seconds
  • My pricing reflects value, not just cost
  • The premium option is shown first
  • I have at least 3 proof elements
  • I've mapped the top 5 customer objections
  • My CTA is one clear obvious next step
Scoring: 7-8 = Strong offer logic. 4-6 = Leaking conversions. 0-3 = Offer is likely the #1 reason sales are hard.
